My Spanish Toolkit

Not everyone starts with classes — and that's totally fine. These are the free tools I actually recommend to my own students. No sign-up, no catch. Just good places to start (or keep going).

🎧 Language Transfer
languagetransfer.org →

"The best free intro to how Spanish actually works. No memorization — it teaches you to think in the language. Starting from zero? Start here."

📺 Dreaming Spanish
youtube.com/@DreamingSpanish →

"Videos you can understand from day one, even as a total beginner. Pick your level, hit play, and let your brain do the work."

🎙️ Radio Ambulante
radioambulante.org →

"Real stories from across Latin America, beautifully told. For intermediate+ learners — and every episode comes with a full transcript, so you can read along. This is where Spanish stops being homework and starts being a world."

📖 SpanishDict
spanishdict.com →

"Forget Google Translate. This is the dictionary my students and I use in every single class. Conjugations, examples, pronunciation — all in one place."

😂 Español con Juan
youtube.com/@EspanolConJuan →

"Juan is funny, a little chaotic, and speaks 100% in Spanish you can actually follow. Proof that learning doesn't have to feel like studying."

🧠 Anki
apps.ankiweb.net →

"The vocabulary tool I trust most. Ten minutes a day beats two hours on Sunday — and Anki makes the ten minutes stick. Pro tip: search 'Spanish' in their shared decks and you'll find thousands ready to go."
